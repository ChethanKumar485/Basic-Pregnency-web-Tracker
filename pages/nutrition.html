🌿

Folic Acid

Prevents neural tube defects. Critical weeks 1–12 for closure. Continue throughout pregnancy for DNA synthesis.

Sources: Leafy greens, lentils, fortified cereals, oranges, avocado, beetroot
400–800 mcg T1 · 600 mcg T2/T3
🩸

Iron

Prevents anaemia. Supports 50% blood volume expansion. Baby builds iron stores in T3 for first 6 months of life.

Sources: Red meat, lentils, ragi, spinach, tofu, liver (limited) + Vitamin C
27 mg/day
🦷

Calcium

Baby's skeleton and teeth. Inadequate intake causes baby to draw from maternal bones, raising osteoporosis risk.

Sources: Dairy, ragi (250mg/100g), sesame seeds, sardines (bones), fortified milks, broccoli
1000 mg/day
🐟

DHA / Omega-3

Brain cortex and retina structural fat. Brain triples in last 13 weeks — DHA critical. Reduces preterm birth risk.

Sources: Salmon, sardines, mackerel (low Hg); walnuts, flaxseed, algae supplements (veg)
200–300 mg DHA/day
🥚

Choline

Brain cell membrane structure. Deficiency causes lifelong memory deficits. Often absent in prenatal vitamins.

Sources: Eggs (best — 2 eggs = 300mg), chicken liver, salmon, soybeans, broccoli, quinoa
450 mg/day
🧂

Iodine

Thyroid hormones regulate fetal brain maturation and neuron development. Leading preventable cause of intellectual disability globally.

Sources: Iodised salt, dairy, eggs, seafood. Vegetarians especially at risk.
220 mcg/day
☀️

Vitamin D

Bone mineralisation with calcium. Deficiency linked to gestational diabetes, preeclampsia, and low birth weight.

Sources: Sunlight (20 min/day), egg yolks, fortified milk, fatty fish, supplements
600–2000 IU/day
💊

Vitamin B12

Nerve development and red blood cell formation. Vegetarians/vegans must supplement — no plant food contains B12.

Sources: Meat, fish, eggs, dairy, fortified plant milks, B12 supplements
2.6 mcg/day
🥦

Magnesium

Regulates blood sugar and blood pressure. Prevents leg cramps common in T2/T3. Supports bone and nerve function.

Sources: Pumpkin seeds, almonds, dark chocolate, spinach, avocado, whole grains
350–360 mg/day
💡 Vegetarian/Vegan Note: Supplement Vitamin B12 (essential — no plant source), monitor iodine, iron (pair with Vitamin C), DHA (algae-based), and zinc. Ragi (finger millet), sesame seeds, and tofu are excellent plant-based calcium sources.

🧠 Brain Development Nutrition

Third trimester (weeks 28–40): 250,000 neurons/minute. Brain triples in weight. Based on systematic reviews in Nutrients, AJCN, and Lancet.

🔬 Critical Window: Weeks 28–40
Rapid synaptogenesis + myelination. DHA, choline, iodine, and iron are the four most critical nutrients. Deficiency in any one causes measurable cognitive differences in offspring.
🍽️ Optimal Brain-Boost Day
Breakfast: 2 eggs + oats + walnuts
Lunch: Salmon + broccoli + iodised salt
Dinner: Dal + spinach + roti
Snack: Walnuts + blueberries

🥚 Choline

Brain membrane structure. Caudill et al. FASEB 2018: maternal supplementation improved infant cognitive processing at 4, 7, 10, and 13 months.

Eggs, beef liver, salmon, chicken breast, soybeans, quinoa, broccoli
450 mg/day

🐟 DHA

Neural membrane structure. Hibbeln et al. Lancet 2007: low DHA associated with lower verbal IQ, social development, and fine motor skills in children.

Salmon, sardines, mackerel; walnuts, flaxseed, chia; algae DHA (vegetarian)
200–300 mg/day

🧂 Iodine

Thyroid hormones regulate neuronal migration and myelination. Bath et al. Lancet 2013: mild deficiency = 8–10 point IQ reduction in offspring.

Iodised salt, dairy, eggs, seafood, fortified bread
220 mcg/day

🩸 Iron

Myelination and dopamine receptors. Lozoff et al. NEJM 1991: iron-deficiency anaemia in pregnancy causes persistent cognitive deficits in offspring.

Red meat, chicken, lentils, ragi, spinach, tofu + Vitamin C
27 mg/day

🫐 Antioxidants

Protect neural tissue from oxidative stress during rapid brain development. Polyphenols in berries cross blood-brain barrier and enhance neuronal signalling.

Blueberries, strawberries, dark leafy greens, bell peppers, dark chocolate (>70%)
Eat daily

🌿 Folate B9

DNA synthesis and cortical neuron production throughout all trimesters. Critical past T1 for brain cell division.

Lentils, chickpeas, spinach, avocado, asparagus, fortified grains, beetroot
600 mcg/day T2/T3
