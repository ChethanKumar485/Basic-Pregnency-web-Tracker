🗓️ Pregnancy Timeline
Trimester-by-trimester checklists, fetal milestones, kick counter, and medical appointments.

Medical Tasks
  • Confirm pregnancy with home test or blood HCG
  • Schedule first OB/midwife appointment (before week 10)
  • Confirmation ultrasound — heartbeat visible by weeks 6–7
  • Lab tests: CBC, blood type, Rh factor, rubella immunity, STI screening
  • Urine culture to check for UTIs
  • Blood pressure, weight baseline recorded
  • Nuchal translucency (NT) scan — weeks 11–14 (chromosomal screening)
  • CVS test if indicated (weeks 10–13)
Personal Preparations
  • Start prenatal vitamins with folic acid (400–800 mcg) daily
  • Record Last Menstrual Period (LMP) to estimate due date
  • Limit caffeine to <200 mg/day (~1 coffee)
  • Eliminate alcohol completely
  • Stop smoking — seek support if needed
  • Review all medications with your provider
  • Schedule dental checkup (hormones affect gums)
  • Review health insurance and maternity coverage
  • Plan when/how to announce pregnancy
💊 Folic acid in the first trimester helps prevent neural tube defects. Start before conception if possible.
Medical Tasks
  • Anatomy scan (level II ultrasound) around week 20 — checks all organs
  • Gestational diabetes screening (glucose challenge test) weeks 24–28
  • Quad screen blood test (weeks 15–20)
  • Amniocentesis if indicated (weeks 15–20)
  • Regular blood pressure monitoring
  • Iron levels checked — supplement if anaemic
  • Get flu vaccine if in season
Personal Preparations
  • Plan and begin setting up nursery
  • Enroll in childbirth education classes
  • Create baby registry
  • Find out baby's sex (if desired) from anatomy scan
  • Start discussing baby names
  • Notify employer and plan maternity leave
  • Maintain 150 min/week moderate activity
  • Begin daily Kegel exercises
🌟 The second trimester is often called the "honeymoon phase" — morning sickness eases and energy returns!
Medical Tasks
  • Get Tdap vaccine (weeks 27–36) — protects newborn from whooping cough
  • Group B Strep (GBS) culture (weeks 35–37)
  • Weekly/biweekly OB appointments starting week 36
  • Biophysical profile (BPP) if high risk
  • Baby in head-down position confirmed by week 34–36
  • Cervical checks for dilation/effacement (week 36+)
Personal Preparations
  • Practice daily kick counts — aim for 10 movements in 2 hours
  • Pack hospital bag by week 36
  • Finalize and share birth plan with provider
  • Install infant car seat and get it inspected
  • Choose a pediatrician
  • Attend breastfeeding class or consult lactation specialist
  • Arrange postpartum support (family, doula, etc.)
  • Sleep on left side (SOS position) for optimal blood flow
⚠️ After 28 weeks, avoid lying flat on your back. This can compress the vena cava and reduce blood flow to you and baby.

Brain Development Highlights

Weeks 1–8

Neural tube closes. Folic acid is critical during this window.

Weeks 18–25

Second trimester: hearing develops; responds to mother's voice. DHA supports auditory neural pathways.

Weeks 28–34

Rapid synaptogenesis — 250,000 neurons/minute! Choline, DHA, iodine are critical now.

Weeks 35–40

Brain triples in weight in last 13 weeks. Myelination begins; omega-3s essential.
