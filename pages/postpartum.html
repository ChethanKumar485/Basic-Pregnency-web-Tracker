Weeks 0–12: The Fourth Trimester

Your body went through an extraordinary change. The postpartum period — weeks 0 to 12 — is a time of physical recovery, emotional adjustment, and bonding. Be patient and kind with yourself.

📅 Postpartum Recovery Timeline
Day 1–3: Hospital / Immediate Recovery

Uterus begins contracting to pre-pregnancy size. Colostrum (first milk) produced. Perineal soreness or C-section incision care. Emotional intensity and "baby blues" may begin.

Days 3–7: Going Home

Breast milk typically comes in days 3–5. Lochia (vaginal discharge) is bright red and heavy. Focus on rest, hydration, and nutrition. Accept help from family.

Week 1–2: "Baby Blues" Window

Up to 80% of mothers experience baby blues — tearfulness, mood swings, anxiety from hormonal shifts. This is normal and usually resolves by 2 weeks.

Week 2–6: Physical Recovery

Lochia changes from red → pink → white/yellow, then stops. Uterus returns to normal size by week 6. C-section scar heals externally. Avoid sexual intercourse and tampon use until cleared.

Week 6: Six-Week Postnatal Checkup ⭐

Critical appointment: physical exam, blood pressure, weight, mental health screen, contraception discussion. Get cleared before resuming full physical activity.

Week 6–12: Gradual Return to Activity

Begin gentle exercise (walking, pelvic floor) after 6-week clearance. C-section mothers may need longer. Running and high-impact exercise typically after 12 weeks with pelvic floor check.

Month 3–6: Hormonal Settling

Hormones continue to fluctuate, especially if breastfeeding. Hair loss (telogen effluvium) common at 3–4 months. Libido and energy gradually return.

💜 Mental Health Screen

Answer honestly. Your feelings matter and postpartum mental health is a medical condition.

Baby Blues (Normal — First 2 Weeks)

  • Mood swings and tearfulness
  • Feeling overwhelmed or anxious
  • Difficulty sleeping even when baby sleeps
  • Irritability or sadness
  • Resolves within 2 weeks without treatment

Postpartum Depression (Needs Treatment)

  • Persistent sadness lasting beyond 2 weeks
  • Feeling disconnected from or unable to bond with baby
  • Feeling like a bad mother or worthless
  • Loss of interest in everything
  • Thoughts of harming yourself or baby
  • Requires medical help — you are not alone

✅ Six-Week Postpartum Checklist
  • Six-week postnatal appointment scheduled
  • Blood pressure checked postpartum
  • Iron/anemia levels rechecked
  • Thyroid function checked (postpartum thyroiditis common)
  • Cervical screening / Pap smear if due
  • Contraception discussed with provider
  • Perineum / C-section healing assessed
  • Mental health screen completed with provider
  • Breastfeeding support / lactation consultation if needed
  • Cleared for exercise / full physical activity
  • Baby's 6-week check with pediatrician
  • Support network in place (family, counsellor, group)
