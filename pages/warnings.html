Head / Neurological

🤯

Severe "Thunderclap" Headache

Sudden, very severe headache that feels like a "clap of thunder" — worst headache of your life. Does not improve with paracetamol.

😵

Persistent Severe Headache

Headache that does not go away with rest or treatment. Could indicate dangerously high blood pressure or preeclampsia.

💫

Dizziness / Fainting

Sudden lightheadedness, loss of balance, or fainting — especially combined with headache or vision changes.

Vision Changes

⚡

Flashing Lights / Floaters

Seeing flashes of light, spots, stars, or visual "auras" that weren't there before. Can signal preeclampsia affecting the brain.

🔵

Blind Spots / Tunnel Vision

Loss of vision in part of your visual field, or vision suddenly narrowing. Seek emergency care immediately.

👁️

Blurred / Double Vision

Vision suddenly becoming blurry or doubled, especially alongside headache or high blood pressure.

Physical / Body Symptoms

🌡️

Fever ≥ 100.4°F / 38°C

Any fever during pregnancy is concerning. High fever can affect fetal neurological development and may indicate infection or chorioamnionitis.

🫁

Trouble Breathing / Chest Pain

Sudden shortness of breath, chest tightness, or pain — especially at rest. Could indicate pulmonary embolism or cardiac issue.

🫀

Rapid / Irregular Heartbeat

Heart racing, fluttering, or skipping beats in a way that feels alarming or doesn't stop.

🤲

Severe Swelling of Hands/Face

Sudden, extreme swelling — especially puffiness around the eyes and face — is a key warning sign of preeclampsia.

🤢

Severe Abdominal / Upper Right Pain

Persistent, severe pain in the upper right abdomen (under the ribs) can indicate HELLP syndrome or liver problems related to preeclampsia.

🤸

Seizure or Convulsions

Any seizure during pregnancy is a medical emergency. Eclampsia (seizure with preeclampsia) is life-threatening. Call 112/911 immediately.

Pregnancy-Specific Signs

🩸

Vaginal Bleeding (More Than Spotting)

Any significant bleeding during pregnancy should be evaluated immediately. Could indicate placenta previa, placental abruption, or miscarriage.

💧

Fluid Leaking / Gush

Sudden gush or steady trickle of clear fluid from the vagina — your water may have broken (PROM). Seek care immediately to prevent infection.

👶

Significant Decrease in Fetal Movement

After week 28, if you feel significantly fewer kicks than usual, or no movement for 2+ hours during your baby's usual active time — seek care immediately.

⏱️

Regular Contractions Before Week 37

Regular, painful contractions before 37 weeks could be preterm labor. 6+ contractions per hour = seek emergency care now.

🦷

Signs of Preeclampsia (Combined)

Combination of: headache + vision changes + swelling + high blood pressure. This is a medical emergency requiring immediate hospitalization.

🔥

Burning / Painful Urination

Signs of UTI — common in pregnancy and can lead to kidney infection (pyelonephritis) if untreated, which can trigger preterm labor.

What is Preeclampsia?

Preeclampsia is a serious pregnancy complication characterized by high blood pressure (≥140/90 mmHg) and signs of damage to organ systems, most often the liver and kidneys. It typically occurs after 20 weeks of pregnancy.

Warning Signs

  • High blood pressure (≥140/90 mmHg)
  • Protein in urine (proteinuria)
  • Severe headache
  • Vision changes (floaters, flashes)
  • Upper right abdominal pain
  • Sudden weight gain from fluid retention
  • Swelling of face and hands

Risk Factors

  • First pregnancy
  • BMI ≥ 30 (obesity)
  • Multiple pregnancy (twins/triplets)
  • Age <20 or >35
  • History of hypertension or kidney disease
  • Previous preeclampsia
  • Diabetes, lupus, or rheumatoid arthritis
⚠️ Preeclampsia can progress to eclampsia (seizures), HELLP syndrome, and organ failure. It is one of the leading causes of maternal mortality. Regular prenatal blood pressure checks are essential.
