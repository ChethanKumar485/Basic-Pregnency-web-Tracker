The SOS Position
Sleep On Side — preferably your LEFT side
Left-side sleeping increases blood and nutrient flow to the placenta, improves kidney function, and reduces ankle swelling. Recommended by ACOG for all trimesters.
Visual Sleeping Position Guide

✓ GOOD: Right Side
🚫 AVOID after Week 28: Back Sleeping (Supine)
💡 HELPFUL: Semi-Reclined (Heartburn/Breathlessness)

⭐⭐⭐ Left Side

Maximum blood flow to placenta and kidneys. Reduces liver pressure. Best for baby's oxygen delivery. Recommended position for all trimesters.

⭐⭐ Right Side

Acceptable alternative. Slight liver compression in late pregnancy but safe. Use if left side is uncomfortable.

⭐ Semi-Reclined

45° angle with pillow props. Excellent for heartburn, acid reflux, and shortness of breath (T3). Safe all trimesters.

🚫 Back Sleeping (after Wk 28)

Baby's weight compresses the inferior vena cava and aorta → reduces blood return to heart, lowers BP, decreases placental flow.

🤰 Pillow Between Knees

Place a pillow between your knees when sleeping on your side. Aligns hips and spine, reducing lower back pain and hip pressure.

All trimesters

🛏️ Pregnancy / Body Pillow

C-shaped or U-shaped pillow supports belly, back, and legs simultaneously. Fewer position changes during the night.

T2 & T3

🔥 Heartburn Relief

Prop upper body at 45° angle with 2–3 pillows. Avoid eating within 2–3 hours of bedtime. Eat small, frequent meals during the day.

T2 & T3

😮‍💨 Shortness of Breath

Uterus grows and presses on diaphragm. Use extra pillows to raise head and upper back. Helps lungs expand more freely.

T3

🦵 Leg Cramps

Stretch calf before bed. Dorsiflex foot (pull toes up) when cramp strikes. Magnesium-rich foods may help. Stay hydrated.

T2 & T3

🚽 Frequent Urination

Reduce fluids 2 hours before bed (maintain daytime hydration). Lean forward when urinating to fully empty bladder.

All trimesters

🌡️ Overheating

Pregnancy raises core temperature. Sleep in a cool room (18–20°C). Use breathable cotton sheets and a lightweight duvet.

All trimesters

😰 Restless Leg Syndrome

Common due to iron/folate deficiency. Light exercise, warm bath before bed, iron-rich foods, and magnesium may reduce symptoms.

T2 & T3

🧠 Anxiety / Insomnia

Common in pregnancy. Try guided meditation, progressive muscle relaxation, journaling worries before bed. Avoid screens 1 hour before sleep.

All trimesters
💤 Pregnancy Sleep Hygiene Checklist
  • Keep consistent sleep and wake times daily
  • Keep bedroom dark, cool (18–20°C), and quiet
  • No screens (phone, TV) for 1 hour before bed
  • No caffeine after 2 PM
  • Nap 20–30 minutes in early afternoon if needed
  • Exercise daily — finish 3+ hours before bedtime
  • Warm (not hot) bath before bed to relax muscles
  • Light snack only — no large meals near bedtime
  • Kegel exercises reduce nighttime urinary urgency
  • Practice deep breathing or meditation before sleep
⚠️ After Week 28: Back Sleeping Warning

Lying flat on your back in late pregnancy compresses the inferior vena cava (large vein returning blood from lower body to heart) and the aorta. This reduces:

  • Blood return to your heart → dizziness, low blood pressure, nausea
  • Blood flow and oxygen delivery to the placenta and baby
  • Kidney and liver blood supply
💡 Don't panic if you wake up on your back. Simply roll to your side. Your body will usually wake you before harm occurs. A wedge pillow behind your back can prevent rolling over during sleep.
